**--- PART 1: IC EVALUATION ---**

**Cell 6 (markdown): Section -- What is the Information Coefficient?**
- Explain IC: Spearman rank correlation between a feature's current value and future returns. IC ranges from -1 to +1. A positive IC means the feature predicts direction; |IC| > 0.02-0.05 is considered meaningful in quant research.
- Explain IC decay: IC typically decreases as the prediction horizon increases. The rate of decay tells us how quickly a signal's predictive power fades.
- Explain IC-IR (Information Ratio): mean(IC) / std(IC) over time -- measures IC stability. IC-IR > 0.5 is a useful signal; > 1.0 is exceptional.

**--- PART 2: PURGED K-FOLD CROSS-VALIDATION ---**

**Cell 18 (markdown): Section -- Why Purged K-Fold?**
- Explain the problem: Standard K-fold CV leaks information in time-series data because temporally adjacent observations share label information (a 5-day forward return computed at bar t overlaps with the return at bar t+1).
- Explain the solution: Purged K-fold removes (purges) training observations whose labels overlap with test observations. An embargo zone adds extra safety buffer.
- "This is essential for unbiased performance estimation in backtesting."

**Cell 19 (code): Build t1 series and create splitter**
